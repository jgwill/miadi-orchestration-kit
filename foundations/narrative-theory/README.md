## Practitioner Adoption (2026)

- **Screenwriters:** Save the Cat dominant; Field's 3-act is baseline at every studio fellowship

**Tension:** Working writers split between *plotters* (beat-following) and *pantsers* (organic, misbelief-first). Most successful use frameworks as diagnostic lens during revision, not pre-write straitjacket.

## Gaps or Cautions

1. **Linear assumption:** Most frameworks inherit Western three-act linearity. Indigenous narrative structures (circular, spiral, balance-as-progress) are underrepresented in canonical theory.
2. **Hero bias:** Campbell's monomyth critiqued for privileging Western/masculine hero journeys. Alternatives (Louise Freeman's "Four Paths") remain niche.
3. **Novelty overclaim:** Every new "beat sheet" or "story structure" borrows from Aristotle + Freytag + Vogler. True innovations are rare; most are repackagings.
4. **Psychology assumption:** Cron's cognitive science foundations are robust but not universalâ€”other narrative traditions (oral, ceremonial, non-Western) may not privilege "misbelief resolution."

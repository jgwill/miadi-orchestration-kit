# Outcome Drift Audit

Use this skill when a draft sounds coherent but you need to know whether it actually satisfies the intended outcomes named in the corpus.

## Outcome anchors to check

- STCKin remains part of the STC bot family.
- STCKin keeps its WEST / integration-reflection position.
- Context-layer language preserves Miadi's downstream consumption relationship to AvaLangStack.
- Promotion decisions respect `provenance -> spec -> wiki`.
- Retrieval/context-layer concerns stay distinct from wiki explanation.
- Outputs are durable enough to support later RISE and wiki drafting waves.

## Audit steps

1. Collect the explicit outcome statements from prompts, reports, rispecs, or wiki pages.
2. Gather the current evidence for each outcome.
3. Mark whether the outcome is:
   - met
   - partial
   - asserted-only
   - contradicted
4. Explain the drift or missing proof.
5. State the consequence for revision and promotion.

## Output format

Produce a table:

| Outcome | Intended source | Evidence found | Status | Drift or gap | Required action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

Then add a short summary:

- strongest drift found
- most important correction
- whether promotion should be blocked
